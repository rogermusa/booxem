GRIT

The Power of Passion and Perseverance

What is Grit? Beyond Talent

Angela Duckworth defines Grit as a unique combination of passion and perseverance applied towards long-term goals. Through extensive research across diverse fields (military training, spelling bees, sales), she found that grit is a more significant predictor of high achievement than talent or IQ.

While talent might determine how quickly skills improve, grit determines the dedication and resilience needed to stick with something long enough to master it and achieve success. Duckworth argues that our culture often overemphasizes innate talent and underestimates the power of sustained effort.

She proposes that effort counts twice in the journey to achievement:
Talent x Effort = Skill
Skill x Effort = Achievement
Therefore, consistent, focused effort is paramount.

"Grit is passion and perseverance for very long-term goals. Grit is having stamina. Grit is sticking with your future, day in, day out, not just for the week, not just for the month, but for years, and working really hard to make that future a reality."

— Angela Duckworth

The Two Components of Grit

Grit isn't just hard work; it's composed of two essential elements:

Passion

This isn't fleeting enthusiasm, but a deep, enduring interest in and commitment to a specific domain or goal. It involves loving what you do and finding it meaningful. Gritty people don't just tolerate their work; they are deeply fascinated and engaged by it over the long haul.

Perseverance

This is the resilience aspect: the ability to stick with goals despite setbacks, failures, adversity, and plateaus in progress. It's about finishing what you start, working diligently day after day, and overcoming obstacles with determination.

"Enthusiasm is common. Endurance is rare."

— Angela Duckworth

Growing Grit from the Inside Out

Duckworth argues that grit isn't a fixed trait; it can be cultivated. She identifies four key psychological assets that gritty individuals tend to possess and develop:

  • 1. Interest: Passion begins with intrinsically enjoying what you do. Discovering and deepening interests is crucial. This often involves exploration followed by development.
  • 2. Practice: Grit requires commitment to continuous improvement through deliberate practice – focusing on specific weaknesses, seeking feedback, and pushing beyond comfort zones. It's about quality of effort, not just quantity.
  • 3. Purpose: Believing that your work matters and contributes to the well-being of others provides profound motivation. Purpose connects your passion to something larger than yourself, fueling long-term perseverance.
  • 4. Hope: This isn't wishful thinking, but the expectation that your own efforts can improve your future. It's closely linked to a growth mindset – the belief that abilities can be developed – enabling resilience in the face of challenges.

Additionally, Duckworth discusses "Growing Grit from the Outside In," highlighting the importance of supportive environments, challenging parenting styles (wise parenting combining warmth and demandingness), extracurricular activities, mentors, and belonging to gritty cultures.

The Decisive Power of Grit

"Grit" provides compelling evidence that passion and perseverance are critical ingredients for outstanding achievement, often outweighing innate talent. It shifts the focus from what we're born with to what we can cultivate through dedication and resilience.

By understanding the components of grit – interest, deliberate practice, purpose, and hope – and recognizing the influence of our environment, we can intentionally develop our own grit and help foster it in others. It's a hopeful message: our capacity for long-term achievement is not fixed.

Success doesn't just come from talent; it comes from showing up, working hard, and sticking with it, especially when things get tough. Cultivate your Passion. Practice your Perseverance. That's the power of Grit.
