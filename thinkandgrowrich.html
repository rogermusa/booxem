Thoughts Are Things: The Philosophy of Achievement

"Think and Grow Rich" presents a philosophy of success based on decades of research into the lives of America's most successful individuals, commissioned by Andrew Carnegie. Napoleon Hill distills their habits and mindsets into actionable principles, arguing that riches begin with a state of mind. What the mind can conceive and believe, it can achieve.

The core idea is that a Burning Desire, crystallized into a definite major purpose and backed by unwavering faith and persistence, can transmute thoughts into their physical equivalent – whether that be financial wealth, career success, or personal achievement. It emphasizes the power of the subconscious mind and the need to direct it purposefully.

Hill asserts that success is not accidental but follows definite laws that anyone can learn and apply. It requires mastering one's thoughts, emotions (especially fear), and actions through self-discipline and collaboration.

The 13 Steps to Riches

Hill outlines thirteen practical steps, derived from the lives of achievers, that form the core of his philosophy:

1
Desire: The Starting Point

Develop a white-hot, burning desire for a specific goal (riches, achievement). It must be definite, not a vague wish. Fixate on exactly what you want, determine what you'll give in return, set a date, create a plan, write it down, and read it twice daily.

2
Faith: Visualizing and Believing

Cultivate unwavering faith in your ability to achieve your desire. Faith is a state of mind induced by autosuggestion and affirmation, acting as the "chemist" of the mind that connects with Infinite Intelligence and gives power to your thoughts.

3
Autosuggestion: Influencing the Subconscious

Use autosuggestion (self-suggestion) to deliberately feed your subconscious mind with thoughts of faith, desire, and your definite purpose. Repeat your written statement of desire aloud, visualizing its attainment with emotion.

4
Specialized Knowledge: Personal Experiences or Observations

Acquire specialized knowledge relevant to your purpose. General knowledge is not enough. Learn continually through experience, education, and association with knowledgeable people (Master Mind). Knowledge is only potential power; it becomes power when organized into definite plans of action.

5
Imagination: The Workshop of the Mind

Utilize both synthetic imagination (rearranging old concepts) and creative imagination (receiving new ideas via hunches/inspiration) to create plans for achieving your desire. Imagination transforms desire into tangible form.

6
Organized Planning: Crystallizing Desire into Action

Transform desire into action through practical, organized plans. Ally yourself with a Master Mind group. Ensure your plans are sound and provide reciprocal benefit. Persistently execute your plans, replacing failed plans immediately with new ones.

7
Decision: The Mastery of Procrastination

Cultivate decisiveness. Successful people make decisions quickly and change them slowly (if ever). Avoid procrastination and being swayed by the opinions of others. Know what you want and stand firm.

8
Persistence: Sustained Effort

Develop persistence – the sustained effort necessary to induce faith. Persistence, based on willpower, overcomes failure and obstacles. It's fueled by desire, self-reliance, definite plans, accurate knowledge, cooperation, habit, and willpower.

9
Power of the Master Mind: The Driving Force

Harness the power generated through a Master Mind alliance – the coordination of knowledge and effort between two or more people working towards a definite purpose in a spirit of harmony. This creates a "third mind" greater than the sum of its parts.

10
The Mystery of Sex Transmutation: Channeling Energy

Learn to transmute the powerful energy of sex drive from physical expression into creative, constructive channels aligned with your purpose. This potent energy, when redirected, fuels imagination, courage, willpower, persistence, and creativity.

11
The Subconscious Mind: The Connecting Link

Understand that the subconscious mind is the intermediary that translates thought impulses (fed by faith and autosuggestion) into their physical equivalent and connects with Infinite Intelligence. Voluntarily influence it with positive emotions.

12
The Brain: Broadcasting and Receiving Station

View the brain as both a broadcasting station, sending out vibrations of thought, and a receiving station, picking up thoughts broadcast by others. The subconscious mind is the sending apparatus; creative imagination is the receiving set.

13
The Sixth Sense: The Door to Infinite Intelligence

Develop the Sixth Sense – the faculty of creative imagination through which the mind connects with Infinite Intelligence, receiving warnings of danger and flashes of inspiration (hunches). It's the apex of the philosophy, mastered only after mastering the other twelve principles.

Key Supporting Concepts

Beyond the steps, Hill emphasizes several supporting ideas:

  • The Master Mind Alliance: Leveraging collective brainpower and harmonious cooperation.
  • The Six Ghosts of Fear: Identifying and conquering the fears of Poverty, Criticism, Ill Health, Loss of Love, Old Age, and Death, which paralyze action.
  • Autosuggestion Techniques: Practical methods for impressing desires onto the subconscious mind through repetition and emotion.
  • Positive Mental Attitude (PMA): Maintaining a mindset focused on faith, hope, and positive expectancy, regardless of temporary setbacks.

The Power to Transmute Thought into Reality

"Think and Grow Rich" remains a cornerstone of personal development literature because its central message is both profound and timeless: your thoughts, when combined with definite purpose, faith, persistence, and planning, have the power to shape your reality.

Hill provides a comprehensive blueprint, not just for accumulating wealth, but for achieving success in any field of endeavor. It requires disciplined thinking, mastery over fear, harnessing subconscious power, and collaborating effectively. The journey starts within your own mind.

Master these principles, apply them consistently, and you can achieve your definite major purpose. Think, believe, plan, persist – and you can Grow Rich.
