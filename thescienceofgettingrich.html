The Foundation: The Thinking Substance

"The Science of Getting Rich," a cornerstone of New Thought literature, posits that there is a **Thinking Substance** (or Formless Stuff) from which all things are made and which permeates all space. This Substance is intelligent, alive, and responds to thought.

The central premise is that **thought**, impressed upon this Formless Substance, creates the form or reality imagined. Therefore, by thinking in a specific way – a "Certain Way" – individuals can cause the creation of riches (defined broadly as having enough to live fully and express potential).

Wattles argues that getting rich is an exact science, like algebra or arithmetic. There are specific laws governing the process of acquiring riches, and if these laws are followed precisely, anyone can become wealthy. It's not about environment, talent (beyond basic ability), or saving pennies, but about thinking and acting according to these universal principles.

"Thought is the only power which can produce tangible riches from the Formless Substance. The stuff from which all things are made is a substance which thinks, and a thought of form in this substance produces the form."

— Wallace D. Wattles

Principles of the Science of Getting Rich

Wattles outlines several fundamental principles for creating wealth:

  • The Right To Be Rich

    It is a natural right and necessity to desire riches, as wealth allows for the fullest expression of one's potential – developing body, mind, and soul. Poverty is not virtuous.

  • Think in the Certain Way

    Hold a clear, detailed mental image of what you desire. Maintain **unwavering faith** that this desire is already on its way to you. Eliminate doubt and fear. Focus thought only on abundance and creation, never on lack or competition.

  • Gratitude

    Cultivate a constant attitude of **gratitude**. Be genuinely thankful for the blessings you already have. Gratitude connects you to the Formless Substance and keeps your mind focused on the positive, attracting more good.

  • Form a Clear Mental Image

    You must have a **clear and definite** picture of what you want to create or acquire. Vague desires produce vague results. Make your mental image precise and hold it continuously.

  • Faith and Purpose

    Combine your clear image with unshakeable **faith** that it is being realized, and a steadfast **purpose** to attain it. This combination impresses your thought upon the Formless Substance.

  • Act in the Certain Way

    Thought must be combined with action. Act **now**, efficiently, and successfully on the tasks of your present environment. Each day, do all that you can do that day. Act with the feeling of already being successful and prosperous. Importantly, act on the **creative plane**, not the competitive one – focus on creating value, not beating others.

  • Increase Life

    Your core motive should be to **increase life** – for yourself and others. Give every person more in use value than you take from them in cash value. Convey the impression of advancement and growth in all interactions.

  • The Impressing Will

    Use your will only upon **yourself** – to maintain your faith, purpose, gratitude, and action in the Certain Way. Do *not* try to use willpower to coerce others or manipulate external circumstances directly; impress your thought on the Formless Substance instead.

"Do all the work you can do, every day, but do it in an efficient manner. Put the power of success, and the purpose to get rich, into everything that you do."

— Wallace D. Wattles

Mastering the Science

"The Science of Getting Rich" offers a concise, powerful formula for achieving wealth based on metaphysical principles and purposeful action. It emphasizes the creative power of thought directed by faith and gratitude, combined with efficient, value-adding action in the present moment.

While written over a century ago, its core message – that mindset, belief, and aligned action are the keys to attracting abundance – continues to resonate and served as a major inspiration for later works like "The Secret." It encourages readers to take control of their mental faculty and act decisively to create the prosperous life they desire.

Think clearly, act purposefully, maintain faith, practice gratitude, and give more in value than you receive. This is the Certain Way that leads to riches.
