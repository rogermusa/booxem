The Golden Circle: Why, How, What

Simon Sinek proposes a simple but powerful model called **The Golden Circle** to explain why some leaders and organizations are so much more influential and inspiring than others. He argues that most organizations communicate from the outside in, focusing first on **WHAT** they do, then maybe **HOW** they do it, but rarely articulating **WHY** they do what they do.

Inspiring leaders and organizations, however, think, act, and communicate from the inside out. They **Start With WHY**. Their "Why" is their purpose, cause, or belief – the fundamental reason they exist beyond making money. This connects with people on an emotional level.

The "How" represents the specific actions, values, or principles that bring the "Why" to life. The "What" is the result of the Why and How – the products, services, or job functions. Sinek asserts that "People don't buy WHAT you do; they buy WHY you do it."

"People don't buy WHAT you do; they buy WHY you do it. And what you do simply proves what you believe."

— Simon Sinek

The Biology Behind the Golden Circle

Sinek links the Golden Circle to the structure of the human brain:

WHAT
HOW HOW
WHY WHY
  • The **WHAT** corresponds to the **Neocortex**, the newest part of our brain responsible for rational thought, analytical processing, and language. It understands facts, figures, features, and benefits.
  • The **HOW** and **WHY** correspond to the **Limbic System**, the older parts of the brain responsible for feelings, trust, loyalty, behavior, and decision-making. It has no capacity for language.

When we communicate the WHAT first, we engage the analytical brain, but it doesn't drive behavior. When we communicate the **WHY**, we tap directly into the limbic brain – the seat of decision-making and emotion. This is why messages starting with WHY resonate deeply and inspire action and loyalty, driving "gut feelings" and trust.

"When we communicate from the outside in, yes, people can understand vast amounts of complicated information like features and benefits and facts and figures. It just doesn’t drive behavior."

— Simon Sinek

Implications: Inspiring Loyalty and Action

Understanding and applying the Start With Why principle has significant implications:

  • Inspiration vs. Manipulation: Marketing based on price, promotions, fear, or novelty (manipulations) can drive transactions but not loyalty. Starting with WHY inspires people who believe what you believe, creating lasting loyalty.
  • Finding Your WHY: The WHY comes from looking backward at your origins, highest values, and core beliefs. It's about purpose, not profit. Profit is a result of a clear WHY.
  • Clarity, Discipline, Consistency: Leaders need **Clarity** of WHY (purpose), **Discipline** of HOW (acting on values), and **Consistency** of WHAT (proving the WHY through actions and results).
  • The Celery Test: A metaphorical test for decision-making. If your WHY is "to be healthy," buying celery makes sense, but buying donuts doesn't. Decisions should align with and filter through the WHY.
  • Attracting Believers: When you communicate your WHY, you attract employees, customers, and partners who share your beliefs. This builds trust and a strong, resilient culture or following.

The Power of Starting with Your Purpose

"Start With Why" provides a compelling framework for understanding what truly drives human connection, loyalty, and inspiration. It challenges conventional thinking that focuses solely on products, features, or tactics.

Whether in business, leadership, or personal life, clarifying your fundamental purpose – your WHY – and communicating it authentically allows you to connect with others on a deeper level, build trust, foster loyalty, and inspire action far more effectively than any manipulation ever could.

Define your WHY. Build your HOW around it. Be consistent in your WHAT. That is the path to lasting influence and fulfillment.
