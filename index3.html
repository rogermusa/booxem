The Origin Story

In 1908, Napoleon Hill was tasked by steel magnate Andrew Carnegie to interview the most successful people of their time to uncover a formula for success.

Over the next 20+ years, Hill interviewed more than 500 wealthy individuals, including Thomas Edison, Alexander Graham Bell, Henry Ford, and Charles M. Schwab, to distill the principles of success.

The result was his 1937 masterpiece "Think and Grow Rich" — one of the best-selling self-help books of all time with over 100 million copies sold worldwide.

"Whatever the mind can conceive and believe, it can achieve."

The 13 Principles for Success

1. Desire

The starting point of all achievement. Not mere wishing, but a burning obsession coupled with a plan and persistence.

2. Faith

Belief and visualization of your desires as already fulfilled. Faith removes limitations and creates self-confidence.

3. Auto-suggestion

The method of influencing your subconscious mind through self-administered suggestions and affirmations.

4. Specialized Knowledge

General knowledge has little value. Organized and specialized knowledge, when applied, is the key to transforming desires into reality.

5. Imagination

The workshop of the mind where plans are created and desires are shaped into reality through synthetic and creative imagination.

6. Organized Planning

Turning desires into concrete plans. Knowledge without action is useless. Create detailed plans and execute them with persistence.

7. Decision

Successful people make decisions quickly and change them slowly. Procrastination and indecision are the major causes of failure.

8. Persistence

The sustained effort necessary to overcome failure. Persistence is to success what carbon is to steel — an essential ingredient.

9. Master Mind

The coordinated knowledge and effort of two or more people working towards a definite purpose. No one succeeds alone.

10. Sex Transmutation

The conversion of sexual energy into creative outlets. Harnessing this powerful drive to fuel achievement in other areas.

11. The Subconscious Mind

The connecting link between the finite mind and Infinite Intelligence. Programming it with positive thoughts is essential.

12. The Brain

A broadcasting and receiving station for thought. The brain can pick up vibrations of thought being released by other brains.

13. The Sixth Sense

The "door" to the temple of wisdom. This is the creative imagination through which hunches and inspirations come.

Six Steps for Transmuting Desire into Gold

1

Fix in your mind the exact amount of money you desire

Be definite about the amount. Don't say "I want plenty of money." Instead, state a precise sum.

2

Determine exactly what you intend to give in return

There is no such reality as "something for nothing." Decide what service or good you will provide in exchange.

3

Establish a definite date for possession

Set a clear deadline for when you intend to have accumulated your desired amount of wealth.

4

Create a definite plan and begin at once

Whether you are ready or not, put your plan into action immediately. Don't wait for the "perfect time."

5

Write a clear, concise statement

Write down the amount, the deadline, what you'll give in return, and your plan for accumulation.

6

Read your statement aloud twice daily

Read once after waking up and once before bed. As you read, visualize yourself already in possession of the money.

Practical Takeaways for Implementation

Daily Affirmation Practice

Write your desires as affirmations and repeat them aloud each morning and evening. Visualize your success as you speak.

Form a Master Mind Alliance

Assemble a group of like-minded individuals who will support your goals and provide diverse perspectives and knowledge.

Create Detailed Written Plans

Don't rely on mental plans. Write down your goals, strategies, and action steps in detail. Review and revise them regularly.

Practice Decisive Action

Train yourself to make decisions quickly but change them slowly. Overcome analysis paralysis by taking prompt action.

Develop Specialized Knowledge

Identify the specific knowledge you need to achieve your goals and create a plan to acquire it through study or experience.

Build Persistence Habits

Set small, achievable goals to build your persistence muscle. Create a daily discipline that reinforces your commitment.
