Life Lessons from Navy SEAL Training

"Make Your Bed" expands on the viral commencement speech Admiral William H. McRaven gave at the University of Texas at Austin. Drawing on his thirty-seven years as a Navy SEAL, he shares ten simple yet profound principles learned during the rigorous SEAL training that helped him overcome challenges not only in his military career but throughout his life.

The core philosophy is that seemingly small, everyday disciplines and attitudes can have a ripple effect, building the character, resilience, and perspective needed to tackle life's larger obstacles and ultimately make a positive impact on the world.

Each chapter focuses on a single lesson, illustrated with compelling anecdotes from McRaven's training and operational experiences, demonstrating how these principles apply universally to achieving personal goals and contributing to a better world.

"If you want to change the world, start off by making your bed."

— Admiral William H. McRaven

The 10 Principles to Change Your Life (and Maybe the World)

McRaven outlines ten core lessons learned from the challenges of SEAL training:

  • 1.

    Start Your Day with a Task Completed.

    Making your bed properly each morning provides a small sense of pride and encourages you to do another task, and another. It reinforces that little things matter and sets a positive tone for the day.

  • 2.

    You Can't Go It Alone.

    Find someone to help you paddle. Teamwork, support, and relying on others are crucial for navigating difficult times. You need people in your life you can depend on.

  • 3.

    Only the Size of Your Heart Matters.

    Measure people by the size of their heart, not the size of their flippers (or physical attributes). Determination, grit, and spirit are more important than innate advantages.

  • 4.

    Life's Not Fair—Drive On!

    Getting over being a "sugar cookie" (punished unfairly for minor infractions) is essential. Sometimes you fail despite your best efforts. Stop complaining, learn from it, and keep moving forward.

  • 5.

    Failure Can Make You Stronger.

    Don't be afraid of the "circus" (extra training for failing). Failure is inevitable and provides valuable lessons. Embrace it as an opportunity to learn, adapt, and grow stronger.

  • 6.

    You Must Dare Greatly.

    Slide down the obstacle headfirst. Taking calculated risks and pushing your boundaries is necessary for significant achievement. Overcoming fear requires courage and action.

  • 7.

    Stand Up to the Bullies.

    Don't back down from the "sharks." Courage isn't absence of fear; it's acting despite it. Confront bullies and oppressive forces with strength and resolve.

  • 8.

    Rise to the Occasion.

    Be your very best in the darkest moments. Adversity reveals true character. Dig deep and perform when stakes are highest.

  • 9.

    Give People Hope.

    Start singing when you're up to your neck in mud. One person can lift the spirits of many. Hope is a powerful force multiplier in difficult times.

  • 10.

    Never, Ever Quit!

    Don't ring the bell. Perseverance and refusing to give up are fundamental to overcoming life's greatest challenges and achieving your goals.

Changing the World, One Task at a Time

"Make Your Bed" offers simple, actionable wisdom forged in the crucible of elite military training. Admiral McRaven translates these demanding experiences into universal lessons applicable to everyday life.

The book emphasizes that discipline, teamwork, resilience, courage, and hope – cultivated through small, consistent actions – are the keys to overcoming adversity, achieving personal success, and making a meaningful difference in the world. It's a call to embrace challenges and live with purpose, starting with the first task of the day.

Start small. Build discipline. Help others. Never quit. These simple acts, repeated daily, can transform your life – and perhaps, even change the world.
