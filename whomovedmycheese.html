The Story: Navigating the Maze of Change

"Who Moved My Cheese?" is a simple parable that reveals profound truths about dealing with change. It features four characters living in a Maze: two mice, Sniff and Scurry, and two "Littlepeople," Hem and Haw. All are searching for **Cheese**, which represents whatever we desire in life – a good job, a loving relationship, money, health, peace of mind, etc. The **Maze** represents where we look for what we want – our workplace, community, or life itself.

Initially, all four characters find a large supply of Cheese at "Cheese Station C." The mice, being simple creatures, notice the supply dwindling and are prepared when it eventually runs out. The Littlepeople, Hem and Haw, become complacent and are shocked and angered when the Cheese disappears.

The story follows their different reactions to this unexpected change, highlighting common human responses to adversity and offering lessons on how to adapt successfully.

The Four Characters & Their Reactions to Change

Each character represents a different approach to change:

Sniff

Anticipates change early by sniffing out trends and noticing small shifts.

Scurry

Doesn't overanalyze; quickly scurries into action when change happens.

Hem

Denies and resists change, fearing it will lead to something worse. Becomes paralyzed by fear and inaction.

Haw

Initially resists like Hem, but eventually learns to adapt when he realizes change can lead to something better. Learns to laugh at his fears and moves forward.

The mice represent the simple, instinctive parts of us that adapt quickly, while the Littlepeople represent the complex, emotional, and analytical parts that can overthink and resist change.

The Handwriting on the Wall: Lessons Learned

As Haw overcomes his fear and ventures into the Maze, he writes key insights on the wall – timeless lessons for navigating change:

  • Change Happens: They keep moving the Cheese.
  • Anticipate Change: Get ready for the Cheese to move.
  • Monitor Change: Smell the Cheese often so you know when it is getting old.
  • Adapt to Change Quickly: The quicker you let go of old Cheese, the sooner you can enjoy new Cheese.
  • Change: Move with the Cheese.
  • Enjoy Change! Savor the adventure and enjoy the taste of new Cheese!
  • Be Ready to Change Quickly and Enjoy It Again: They keep moving the Cheese.

Finding Your New Cheese

"Who Moved My Cheese?" offers a simple yet powerful allegory for accepting and adapting to the inevitable changes in work and life. It encourages readers to identify which character they most resemble and to adopt the more adaptive behaviors of Sniff, Scurry, and eventually, Haw.

The key message is to anticipate change, let go of the past quickly, overcome fear by taking action, and embrace the potential benefits that change can bring. By moving with the "Cheese," we open ourselves up to new opportunities and ultimately find greater success and happiness.

Don't get stuck lamenting the loss of old Cheese. Ask yourself, "What would I do if I weren't afraid?" and venture out into the Maze to find something better.
