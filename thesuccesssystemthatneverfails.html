Success is Achievable: PMA + Action

W. Clement Stone, a self-made millionaire and philanthropist heavily influenced by Napoleon Hill, presents his "Success System That Never Fails." The core philosophy is remarkably straightforward yet powerful: success in any endeavor is not a matter of luck or inherent talent, but the result of applying a specific, repeatable system grounded in a Positive Mental Attitude (PMA) combined with consistent action towards a Definite Major Purpose (DMP).

Stone argues that the starting point for all achievement is cultivating and maintaining PMA – an optimistic, confident, and resilient mindset that expects success and sees opportunity even in adversity. This positive mental state must then be directed towards a clearly defined goal (DMP) and fueled by inspiration to action.

The book emphasizes that this system is accessible to everyone, regardless of their background or current circumstances. It provides practical steps and motivational insights drawn from Stone's own extraordinary life and the principles he taught.

"Whatever the mind can conceive and believe, the mind can achieve regardless of how many times you may have failed in the past or how lofty your aims and hopes may be."

— W. Clement Stone

Components of the Success System

Stone's system integrates several key elements, many echoing the work of Napoleon Hill:

  • Positive Mental Attitude (PMA)

    The cornerstone of the system. PMA involves maintaining optimism, faith, enthusiasm, and courage. It's about consciously choosing positive thoughts and interpretations, especially when facing challenges. Regularly using affirmations like "Do it now!" helps cultivate PMA.

  • Definite Major Purpose (DMP)

    Having a clear, specific, written-down primary goal provides direction and focus for your energy and actions. Knowing exactly what you want is crucial for mobilizing your resources and maintaining motivation.

  • Going the Extra Mile

    Consistently doing more than you are paid for or expected to do. This habit builds goodwill, increases your value, creates opportunities, and develops personal initiative and positive momentum.

  • Applied Faith & Specialized Knowledge

    Believing in your ability to achieve your DMP and acquiring the necessary specialized knowledge through learning and experience. Faith acts as a catalyst, while knowledge provides the tools.

  • Master Mind Alliance

    Collaborating harmoniously with others who share similar goals or possess complementary skills. This creates synergy and provides support, knowledge, and accountability.

  • Action Orientation ("Do It Now!")

    Overcoming procrastination and inertia by taking immediate, consistent action towards your goals. Stone's famous motto, "Do It Now!", encapsulates this principle.

  • Controlled Enthusiasm

    Generating genuine enthusiasm for your goals and plans. Enthusiasm is contagious and fuels persistence and inspires others.

  • Accurate Thinking & Learning from Defeat

    Separating facts from opinions, thinking logically, and, crucially, viewing temporary defeat not as failure but as an opportunity to learn, adjust plans, and strengthen resolve.

Key Ideas for Implementation

Stone provides practical advice for putting the system into action:

  • Seek Inspiration: Actively look for motivation in books, talks, and successful people.
  • Use Repetition: Embed principles through repeated affirmations and actions.
  • Employ Autosuggestion: Consciously program your subconscious with positive beliefs.
  • Cultivate Personal Initiative: Be a self-starter; don't wait to be told what to do.
  • Share the Wealth: Believe in giving back and helping others succeed.
  • Recognize Cycles: Understand that success often involves ups and downs; maintain PMA through cycles.
  • Self-Discipline: Master your thoughts, emotions, and actions.
  • Universality: Believe the system works for anyone willing to apply it.

"The Success System That Never Fails" offers a timeless and optimistic blueprint for achieving success based on universal principles of mindset and action. W.
